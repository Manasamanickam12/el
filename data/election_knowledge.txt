The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) symbol is the lotus. Its key leader is Narendra Modi.
The Indian National Congress (INC) symbol is the hand. Its key leader is Mallikarjun Kharge.
The Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) symbol is the Rising Sun. Its key leader is M.K. Stalin.
The All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (ADMK or AIADMK) symbol is the Two Leaves. Its key leader is Edappadi K. Palaniswami.
The Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK) is a new party founded by actor Vijay.

India conducts general elections (Lok Sabha) every 5 years to elect the Prime Minister.
The Election Commission of India (ECI) is responsible for conducting fair elections.
Voting in India is done using Electronic Voting Machines (EVM) and VVPAT.
To vote in India, a person must be at least 18 years old and have a Voter ID.
State Assembly elections are held to elect the Chief Minister of a state.
The model code of conduct is a set of guidelines issued by the ECI for political parties and candidates.
The total number of Lok Sabha seats is 543.
The majority mark to form a government at the center is 272 seats.
General Elections in India are a massive democratic process involving millions of voters.
Political parties use symbols to help illiterate voters identify them on the ballot.
Candidates must file nomination papers and declare their assets before contesting.
Campaigning usually stops 48 hours before the voting begins (silence period).
Counting of votes is done on a specific day assigned by the Election Commission.
The results are declared official after certificates are issued to the winning candidates.
